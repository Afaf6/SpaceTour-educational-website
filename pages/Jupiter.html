Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system — more than twice as massive as all other planets combined.
It is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium and has no solid surface.

Its famous Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been active for over 300 years.
Jupiter has more than 90 moons, including Europa, which may contain an underground ocean.
Average temperature in upper clouds: about −145°C.
