Venus

Venus is known as Earth’s twin because of its similar size.
However, it is the hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere that traps heat.
Its surface is covered with volcanoes and rocky plains.

Venus is often called Earth’s “sister planet” because it is similar in size and structure.
However, it is the hottest planet in the solar system due to a powerful greenhouse effect caused by its thick carbon-dioxide atmosphere. Surface temperatures reach about 465°C, hot enough to melt lead.

The planet is covered by dense clouds of sulfuric acid, which reflect sunlight and make Venus the brightest planet visible from Earth.
A strange fact about Venus is that it rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction compared to most planets.
