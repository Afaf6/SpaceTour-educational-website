Mars

Mars, also called the Red Planet, gets its color from iron-rich dust.
Scientists believe Mars once had water on its surface.
It is one of the most explored planets by space missions.

Mars is known as the “Red Planet” because of iron oxide (rust) covering its surface.
Scientists believe Mars once had rivers, lakes, and possibly oceans billions of years ago. Today, it is cold and dry, with average temperatures around −60°C.

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and massive dust storms that can cover the entire planet.
Because of similarities to early Earth, Mars is a major target for future human exploration.
